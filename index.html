Why Is Substance Screening Necessary?

Substance abuse has taken on alarming proportions in South Africa. Current statistics indicate that substance abuse in South Africa is twice the world norm, rendering the country among the top ten narcotics and alcohol abusers in the world.

In a study released by the Soul City Institute for Social Justice in 2016, it is estimated that substance abuse costs the South African economy 6.4% of GDP which amounts to approximately R136 billion per year!

The knock-on effect on the economy and businesses both small and large, has huge financial implications. Employees who struggle with substance abuse problems result in absenteeism, poor job performance, and a failure to meet set deadlines. Added to this, employees that abuse substances can also pose a very real safety risk to other employees. Criminal activities, such as theft and fraud, is also a risk for many companies struggling with employees that abuse substances.

The Occupational Health and Safety Act (Act 85 of 1993) is clear that employers are liable for managing the negative impact of substance abuse in the workplace. Per the Act, all employers must ensure a substance-free workplace, the absence of which, can present an occupational hazard.
